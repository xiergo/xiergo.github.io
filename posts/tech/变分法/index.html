变分法

  • 证明：Euler-Lagrange Equation

变分法可以用来求泛函的极值问题，即：

找一个满足边界条件$y(x_1)=y_1;y(x_2)=y_2$的函数$y=y(x)$使得泛函$I=\int_{x_1}^{x_2}F(x,y,y’)\text dx$达到极值点。

这就是欧拉-拉格朗日方程解决的问题。

证明：Euler-Lagrange Equation#

解：

设$y=f(x)$满足边界条件： $$ y(x_1)=y_1\\ y(x_2)=y_2\\ $$ 且泛函$I=\int_{x_1}^{x_2}F(x,y,y’)dx$达到极值点

引入$\eta(x)$满足$\eta(x_1)=\eta(x_2)=0$且有连续二阶导，则$\bar y(x)=y(x)+\varepsilon \eta(x)$代表一类满足边界条件的函数族。

这里$\varepsilon\eta(x)$就是$y(x)$的微小变化量，称做$y(x)$的变分（variational），记作$\delta y$。

那么问题变成找一个特定的$\bar y(x)$使得$I(\varepsilon)=\int_{x_1}^{x_2}F(x,\bar y, \bar y’)$达到极值点。因为$\varepsilon=0$是$I(\varepsilon)$的极值点，所以： $$ \frac{\text dI}{\text d\varepsilon}\bigg \vert_{\varepsilon=0}=0\\ $$ $$ \frac{\text d}{\text d \varepsilon} \bigg \vert_{\varepsilon=0} \int_{x_1}^{x_2}F(x,\bar y, \bar y’)\text dx=0\\ $$ $$ \int_{x_1}^{x_2}\frac{\partial F(x,\bar y, \bar y’)}{\partial \varepsilon}\bigg \vert_{\varepsilon=0}\text dx=0\\ $$ $$ \int_{x_1}^{x_2}\bigg [\frac{\partial F}{\partial \bar y}\eta+\frac{\partial F}{\partial \bar y’}\eta’\bigg ]\bigg \vert_{\varepsilon=0}\text dx=0 \tag{1} $$

由分部积分可得

$$ \begin{aligned} &\int_{x_1}^{x_2}\frac{\partial F}{\partial \bar y’}\eta’\text dx\\ =&\int_{x_1}^{x_2}\frac{\partial F}{\partial \bar y’}\text d\eta\\ =&-\int_{x_1}^{x_2}\eta\frac{\text d}{\text d x}\bigg [\frac{\partial F}{\partial \bar y’}\bigg ]\text dx+\frac{\partial F}{\partial \bar y’}\eta \bigg\vert_{x_1}^{x_2}\\ =&-\int_{x_1}^{x_2}\eta\frac{\text d}{\text d x}\bigg [\frac{\partial F}{\partial \bar y’}\bigg ]\text dx \end{aligned} $$

代入(1)式，则 $$ \int_{x_1}^{x_2}\bigg [\frac{\partial F}{\partial \bar y}-\frac{\text d}{\text dx}\bigg(\frac{\partial F}{\partial \bar y’}\bigg)\bigg ]\eta\bigg \vert_{\varepsilon=0}\text dx=0 $$ 在$\varepsilon=0$时，$\bar y = y,\bar y’= y’$，则： $$ \int_{x_1}^{x_2}\bigg [\frac{\partial F}{\partial y}-\frac{\text d}{\text dx}\bigg(\frac{\partial F}{\partial y’}\bigg)\bigg ]\eta\text dx=0 $$ 因为上式对$\forall \eta(x)$都成立，因此 $$ \bigg[\frac{\partial F}{\partial y}-\frac{\text d}{\text dx}\bigg(\frac{\partial F}{\partial y’}\bigg)\bigg ]=0 \tag{\text{Euler-Lagrange Equation}} $$
